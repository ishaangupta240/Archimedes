Claw of Archimedes

An ancient weapon devised by Archimedes to defend the seaward portion of Syracuse's city wall against amphibious assault






Archimedes Screw

The Archimedes screw is made up of a hollow cylinder and a spiral part. One end is placed in a low-lying fluid source and the other end is tilted up into a higher discharge area.





Burning mirrors

A large convex lens that can concentrate the sun's rays onto a small area, heating up the area and thus resulting in ignition of the exposed surface.
